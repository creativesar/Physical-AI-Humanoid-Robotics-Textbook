# Textbook Content Outline: Modules 5-19

## Module 5: Robot Kinematics and Dynamics
*   **5.1 Forward Kinematics:** Denavit-Hartenberg parameters, transformation matrices
*   **5.2 Inverse Kinematics:** Analytical and numerical solutions, redundancy
*   **5.3 Robot Dynamics:** Lagrangian and Newton-Euler formulations, rigid body dynamics
*   **5.4 Trajectory Planning:** Joint space vs. task space trajectories, path generation

## Module 6: Robot Control Systems
*   **6.1 PID Control:** Principles, tuning methods, limitations
*   **6.2 Advanced Control Techniques:** Adaptive control, robust control, optimal control
*   **6.3 Force and Impedance Control:** Interaction with the environment, compliance
*   **6.4 Actuator Control:** Motor control, sensor feedback

## Module 7: Robot Sensing and Perception
*   **7.1 Proprioceptive Sensors:** Encoders, IMUs, force/torque sensors
*   **7.2 Exteroceptive Sensors:** Cameras, LiDAR, ultrasonic sensors
*   **7.3 Sensor Fusion:** Kalman filters, particle filters, data integration
*   **7.4 Computer Vision for Robotics:** Object detection, tracking, pose estimation

## Module 8: Robot Navigation and Localization
*   **8.1 Localization:** Kidnapped robot problem, Monte Carlo localization
*   **8.2 Mapping:** Occupancy grids, SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping)
*   **8.3 Path Planning:** A*, Dijkstra, RRT, motion primitives
*   **8.4 Obstacle Avoidance:** Potential fields, dynamic window approach

## Module 9: Human-Robot Interaction (HRI)
*   **9.1 HRI Principles:** Safety, communication, collaboration
*   **9.2 Haptic Feedback:** Touch and force interaction
*   **9.3 Speech and Gesture Recognition:** Natural language understanding
*   **9.4 Ethical Considerations in HRI:** Trust, autonomy, responsibility

## Module 10: Robot Learning (Reinforcement Learning for Robotics)
*   **10.1 Basics of Reinforcement Learning:** Markov Decision Processes, policies, value functions
*   **10.2 Model-Free RL:** Q-learning, SARSA, Deep Q-Networks (DQN)
*   **10.3 Model-Based RL:** Dyna, policy search
*   **10.4 Application to Robotics:** Locomotion, manipulation, task learning

## Module 11: Humanoid Robot Actuation and Mechanics
*   **11.1 Humanoid Leg Mechanisms:** Bipedal locomotion, balance control
*   **11.2 Humanoid Arm and Hand Mechanisms:** Grasping, manipulation, dexterity
*   **11.3 Power Systems:** Batteries, power distribution, energy efficiency
*   **11.4 Materials and Fabrication:** Lightweight design, compliant structures

## Module 12: Humanoid Robot Locomotion
*   **12.1 Bipedal Walking:** Zero Moment Point (ZMP), gait generation
*   **12.2 Dynamic Walking:** Capture point, push recovery
*   **12.3 Running and Jumping:** Advanced locomotion
*   **12.4 Whole-Body Control for Locomotion:** Coordination of multiple joints

## Module 13: Humanoid Robot Manipulation
*   **13.1 Grasp Planning:** Force closure, form closure, compliant grasping
*   **13.2 Object Manipulation:** Dexterous manipulation, dual-arm manipulation
*   **13.3 Tool Use:** Adapting to tools, human-like tool interaction
*   **13.4 Learning from Demonstration for Manipulation:** Imitation learning

## Module 14: AI for Decision Making in Humanoids
*   **14.1 Cognitive Architectures:** SOAR, ACT-R, intelligent control
*   **14.2 Planning and Reasoning:** Task planning, hierarchical planning, knowledge representation
*   **14.3 Emotion and Social Robotics:** Expressing and understanding emotions, social cues
*   **14.4 Learning and Adaptation:** Lifelong learning, skill acquisition

## Module 15: Soft Robotics and Bio-inspired Design
*   **15.1 Principles of Soft Robotics:** Compliance, adaptability, safety
*   **15.2 Soft Actuators:** Pneumatic, hydraulic, electroactive polymers
*   **15.3 Soft Sensors:** Stretchable sensors, tactile sensing
*   **15.4 Bio-inspired Locomotion and Manipulation:** Mimicking nature

## Module 16: Ethical and Societal Implications of Humanoid Robotics
*   **16.1 Safety and Responsibility:** Autonomous systems, liability
*   **16.2 Employment and Economy:** Automation, job displacement
*   **16.3 Privacy and Data Security:** Data collection, surveillance
*   **16.4 Legal and Regulatory Frameworks:** Governance, standards

## Module 17: Advanced Humanoid Robot Platforms
*   **17.1 Atlas (Boston Dynamics):** Dynamics, control, advanced behaviors
*   **17.2 HRP Series (Kawada Robotics):** Human-scale, industrial applications
*   **17.3 Valkyrie (NASA):** Disaster relief, space exploration
*   **17.4 Other Emerging Platforms:** Research and development

## Module 18: Future Trends in Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics
*   **18.1 General Purpose Humanoid Robots:** Towards versatile assistants
*   **18.2 AI-driven Design and Fabrication:** Generative design, automated manufacturing
*   **18.3 Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCI) for Robotics:** Direct control
*   **18.4 Cloud Robotics and Swarm Intelligence:** Distributed systems, collective behavior

## Module 19: Project-Based Learning and Advanced Topics
*   **19.1 Robotics Simulation Environments:** Gazebo, CoppeliaSim, MuJoCo
*   **19.2 Open-Source Robotics Frameworks:** ROS (Robot Operating System), OpenRAVE
*   **19.3 Advanced Research Topics:** Quantum robotics, consciousness in AI (brief overview)
*   **19.4 Capstone Projects:** Designing and implementing a humanoid robot task
