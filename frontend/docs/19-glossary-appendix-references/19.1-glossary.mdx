## Glossary

This glossary provides definitions for key terms and concepts used throughout the "Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics Textbook." Understanding these terms is essential for grasping the material presented in each module.

**A**

- **Actuator:** A component of a machine that is responsible for moving and controlling a mechanism or system.
- **AI (Artificial Intelligence):** The simulation of human intelligence processes by machines, especially computer systems.
- **AMCL (Adaptive Monte Carlo Localization):** A probabilistic localization algorithm used in ROS 2's Nav2 stack.

**B**

- **Bipedal Locomotion:** Movement using two legs.
- **Build System:** Tools that automate the creation of executable applications from source code.

**C**

- **Calibration:** The process of adjusting a sensor or device to improve its accuracy.
- **Capstone Project:** A culminating academic experience for students to demonstrate their knowledge and skills.
- **CSI (Camera Serial Interface):** A high-speed interface specifically designed for cameras on embedded systems like Jetson.
- **CUDA:** NVIDIA's parallel computing platform and programming model for GPUs.
- **cuDNN:** NVIDIA's GPU-accelerated library for deep neural networks.

**D**

- **DDS (Data Distribution Service):** A middleware standard used by ROS 2 for publish-subscribe communication.
- **Depth Camera:** A camera that provides distance information to objects in its field of view, in addition to color.
- **Digital Twin:** A virtual model designed to accurately reflect a physical object.
- **DH Parameters (Denavit-Hartenberg Parameters):** A convention for describing the kinematic structure of robotic manipulators.
- **Domain Randomization:** A technique in simulation to vary non-essential properties of a training environment to improve generalization to the real world.
- **DWA (Dynamic Window Approach):** A local planning algorithm used in Nav2 for obstacle avoidance.
- **Dynamics:** The study of motion and the forces that cause it.

**E**

- **Edge AI:** Artificial intelligence processing performed directly on the device where data is collected, rather than in a centralized cloud server.
- **Embodied Intelligence:** Intelligence that arises from the interaction of a physical body with its environment.
- **End-Effector:** The part of a robot that interacts with the environment (e.g., a gripper, hand, or tool).
- **E-Stop (Emergency Stop):** A safety mechanism to immediately halt robot operation in case of an emergency.
- **Extrinsic Calibration:** Determining the pose of a sensor relative to a known coordinate frame.

**F**

- **FastAPI:** A modern, fast (high-performance) web framework for building APIs with Python.
- **Feature Extraction:** The process of identifying and describing distinctive points or regions in an image for visual processing.
- **Forward Kinematics (FK):** The calculation of the position and orientation of the end-effector from the joint angles.
- **Force:** A push or pull that can cause an object to accelerate.

**G**

- **Gait Planning:** The process of generating a sequence of motions for a bipedal robot to achieve stable and efficient locomotion.
- **Gazebo:** A powerful 3D robotics simulator.
- **Global Planner:** In navigation, an algorithm that generates a high-level, long-term path to a goal.
- **GPU (Graphics Processing Unit):** A specialized electronic circuit designed to rapidly manipulate and alter memory to accelerate the creation of images, large language models, or other computationally intensive tasks.

**H**

- **Homogeneous Transformation Matrix:** A 4x4 matrix combining rotation and translation, used to represent the pose of a coordinate frame.
- **Humanoid Robot:** A robot with a body shape built to resemble the human body.
- **Human-Robot Interaction (HRI):** The study of interactions between humans and robots.

**I**

- **I2C (Inter-Integrated Circuit):** A serial computer bus often used for attaching low-speed peripheral ICs to processors.
- **IMU (Inertial Measurement Unit):** A sensor that measures and reports a body's specific force, angular rate, and sometimes the orientation.
- **Inertial Model:** Describes the mass and rotational inertia of a robot link.
- **Inverse Kinematics (IK):** The calculation of the joint angles required to achieve a desired end-effector pose.
- **Isaac ROS:** A collection of hardware-accelerated ROS 2 packages developed by NVIDIA for high-performance robotics applications.
- **Isaac Sim:** NVIDIA's photorealistic, physics-based robotics simulator built on the Omniverse platform.
- **Intrinsic Calibration:** Determining the internal parameters of a sensor (e.g., camera focal length).

**J**

- **Jacobian Matrix:** A matrix that relates joint velocities to end-effector linear and angular velocities.
- **Jetson:** NVIDIA's series of embedded computing boards for AI at the edge.
- **Joint:** A connection between two rigid bodies (links) in a robot that allows relative motion.
- **Joint Limits:** Physical constraints on the range of motion of a robot joint.

**K**

- **Kalman Filter:** A powerful mathematical tool for sensor fusion that estimates the state of a dynamic system from noisy measurements.
- **Kinematics:** The study of motion without considering the forces that cause it.
- **Kinematic Chain:** A sequence of rigid bodies (links) connected by joints.

**L**

- **Launch File:** A script in ROS 2 that automates the process of starting and configuring multiple nodes and their parameters.
- **LiDAR (Light Detection and Ranging):** A remote sensing method that uses pulsed laser light to measure ranges.
- **Link:** A rigid body part of a robot.
- **Localization:** The process of determining a robot's pose within a map.
- **Loop Closure:** In SLAM, the process of detecting when a robot returns to a previously visited location to correct accumulated drift.
- **Local Planner:** In navigation, an algorithm that generates short-term, collision-free trajectories while avoiding local obstacles.

**M**

- **Magnetometer:** A sensor that measures magnetic field strength and direction.
- **Mapping:** The process of creating a spatial representation of the environment.
- **MDX (Markdown with JSX):** A file format allowing for rich, interactive components alongside standard Markdown.
- **Mermaid:** A JavaScript-based diagramming tool that uses Markdown-inspired text definitions to create diagrams.
- **Model Predictive Control (MPC):** An advanced control technique that uses a dynamic model to predict future behavior and optimize control actions.
- **Motion Retargeting:** Transferring a motion from one character or robot model to another.
- **Multimodal AI:** AI models that process and integrate information from multiple data types (modalities), such as vision and language.

**N**

- **Nav2 (ROS 2 Navigation Stack):** A comprehensive framework for autonomous mobile robot navigation.
- **Node:** An executable process in ROS 2 that performs computation.
- **NTP (Network Time Protocol):** A networking protocol for clock synchronization between computer systems.

**O**

- **Obstacle Avoidance:** The ability of a robot to detect and safely navigate around obstacles.
- **Occupancy Grid Map:** A 2D grid map representing the probability of cells being occupied, free, or unknown.
- **Odometry:** The use of data from motion sensors to estimate the change in position over time.
- **Omniverse:** NVIDIA's collaborative platform for 3D content creation and simulation.
- **ORB-SLAM3:** A versatile and robust SLAM system.

**P**

- **Package:** The fundamental unit of organization in ROS 2, containing related files like nodes and launch files.
- **Perception Layer:** The part of a robot's architecture responsible for interpreting sensor data to understand the environment.
- **Physical AI:** Artificial Intelligence applied to physical systems and robots that interact with the real world.
- **PID Controller (Proportional-Integral-Derivative):** A common feedback control loop mechanism.
- **Point Cloud:** A set of data points in a three-dimensional coordinate system.
- **Policy:** In reinforcement learning, a mapping from states to actions that an agent takes.
- **Pose Estimation:** Determining the position and orientation of an object or camera.
- **Power Management:** Strategies and techniques for optimizing energy consumption and performance.
- **Prismatic Joint:** A joint that allows linear motion along a single axis.
- **Publisher/Subscriber:** A messaging pattern where senders (publishers) send messages without knowing who will receive them, and receivers (subscribers) receive messages without knowing who sent them.

**Q**

- **Qdrant:** A vector similarity search engine and vector database.

**R**

- **RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation):** An AI technique that enhances language models by retrieving information from external knowledge bases.
- **Reality Gap:** The discrepancy between simulation and the real world that hinders direct transfer of policies.
- **Reinforcement Learning (RL):** A machine learning paradigm where an agent learns to make decisions by performing actions in an environment to maximize a cumulative reward.
- **Revolute Joint:** A joint that allows rotational motion around a single axis.
- **RGB-D Camera:** A camera that captures both color (RGB) and depth information.
- **Rigging:** The process of creating a skeletal system for a 3D model for animation.
- **ROS 2 (Robot Operating System 2):** An open-source, flexible framework for writing robot software.
- **`ros_gz_bridge`:** A ROS 2 package that facilitates communication between ROS 2 and Gazebo.
- **RViz:** A 3D visualization tool for ROS 2.

**S**

- **Safety Layer:** A dedicated part of a robot's architecture ensuring safe operation and interaction.
- **SDF (Simulation Description Format):** An XML-based file format used by Gazebo to describe entire simulation worlds.
- **Sensor Fusion:** Combining data from multiple sensors to achieve a more accurate and reliable estimate.
- **Service (Request/Response):** A ROS 2 communication pattern for synchronous, one-to-one interactions.
- **Sim2Real (Simulation-to-Real Transfer):** The process of deploying policies or models trained in simulation to physical robots.
- **SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping):** The computational problem of concurrently building a map of an unknown environment while tracking an agent's location within it.
- **`slam_toolbox`:** A robust 2D SLAM solution for ROS 2.
- **SPI (Serial Peripheral Interface):** A synchronous serial communication interface.
- **Stability:** The ability of a robot to maintain balance and avoid falling.
- **Support Polygon:** The convex hull of all the points where a robot is in contact with the ground.
- **System Identification:** The process of building mathematical models of dynamic systems from observed data.

**T**

- **Task Grounding:** Connecting abstract language descriptions to concrete perceptions and actions in the physical world.
- **TEB (Timed-Elastic Band):** A local planning algorithm used in Nav2 for obstacle avoidance and path optimization.
- **TF2 (Transformations):** The ROS 2 library for managing coordinate frames.
- **Thermal Management:** Controlling heat generated by electronic components to prevent overheating.
- **Topic:** A named bus over which nodes exchange messages in ROS 2 (publisher/subscriber pattern).
- **Torque:** A twisting force that causes rotation.
- **Trajectory Optimization:** Generating an optimal path or motion for a robot.

**U**

- **Unity:** A popular game development platform also used for robotics simulation.
- **URDF (Unified Robot Description Format):** An XML-based file format in ROS to describe robot models.
- **UART (Universal Asynchronous Receiver/Transmitter):** A common serial communication interface.

**V**

- **VINS-Mono:** A robust and accurate visual-inertial SLAM system for monocular cameras.
- **VLA (Vision-Language-Action) Models:** AI models that enable robots to understand natural language commands and perform actions based on visual and linguistic inputs.
- **VSLAM (Visual SLAM):** SLAM specifically using camera data.

**W**

- **Whole-Body Control:** A control strategy for complex robots that coordinates the motion of all joints to achieve multiple tasks simultaneously.
- **Workstation:** A high-performance computer designed for technical or scientific applications.

**X**

- **XACRO (XML Macros):** A macro language used to simplify the creation of URDF files.

**Z**

- **Zero Moment Point (ZMP):** The point on the ground where the net moment of the inertial forces and gravity is zero, crucial for dynamic stability.
- **Zero-Shot Classification:** The ability of a model to classify objects it has not been explicitly trained on.
