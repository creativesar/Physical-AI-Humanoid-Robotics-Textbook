## 1. What is a Policy?

In robotics and reinforcement learning, a policy defines the behavior of an agent. It's a mapping from observed states of the environment to actions that the agent should take. Essentially, it's the "brain" of the robot that dictates how it should act.

## 2. Approaches to Policy Learning

Policies can be learned using various techniques:
- **Reinforcement Learning (RL):** The robot learns by trial and error, optimizing its actions to maximize a reward signal.
- **Imitation Learning (Learning from Demonstration):** The robot learns by observing human demonstrations of the desired task.
- **Behavior Cloning:** A form of imitation learning where a neural network directly maps observations to actions based on expert data.
