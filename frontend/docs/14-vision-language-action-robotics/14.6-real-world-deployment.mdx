## 1. Introduction to Real-World VLA Deployment

Deploying Vision-Language-Action (VLA) robotics systems from research labs and simulations into real-world environments presents a new set of complex challenges. The controlled conditions of a lab or simulator often fail to capture the full spectrum of variability, noise, and unexpected events present in the real world.

## 2. Bridging the Reality Gap for VLA

The "reality gap" is even more pronounced for VLA systems, which rely heavily on accurate perception and robust language understanding. Techniques to bridge this gap include:
- **Extensive Domain Randomization:** Applying diverse randomization in simulation during training for better generalization.
- **Sim2Real Transfer Learning:** Using advanced transfer learning techniques to adapt models trained in simulation to real-world data.
- **Robust Perception Models:** Training perception models on diverse real-world datasets with various lighting, occlusions, and clutter.
