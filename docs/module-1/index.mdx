# Introduction to Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics

## Overview

Welcome to the comprehensive textbook on Physical AI and Humanoid Robotics. This module provides an introduction to the fundamental concepts that form the foundation of this interdisciplinary field.

## Learning Objectives

- Recognize the interdisciplinary nature of the field

## What is Physical AI?

Physical AI refers to the field of artificial intelligence that is grounded in physical interaction with the environment. Unlike traditional AI systems that operate primarily in digital domains, Physical AI systems must navigate, understand, and manipulate the physical world.

### Key Characteristics

- **Embodiment**: Intelligence emerges through interaction with the physical world
- **Real-time Processing**: Systems must respond to dynamic physical environments
- **Sensorimotor Integration**: Close coupling between perception and action
- **Energy Efficiency**: Physical constraints guide design and operation

## The Humanoid Approach

Humanoid robots serve as ideal platforms for exploring Physical AI because they:

1. Share the same operating environment as humans
2. Can leverage human-designed tools and infrastructure
3. Enable intuitive interaction with human operators
4. Provide insights into human motor control and cognition

## Glossary

- **Embodiment**: The concept that intelligence emerges through interaction between an agent and its environment
- **Physical AI**: Artificial intelligence systems grounded in physical interaction with the real world
- **Sensorimotor**: The integration of sensory input and motor output in control systems
- **Humanoid**: A robot designed with human-like characteristics, typically including a head, torso, arms, and legs
